Introduction

Nelumbo nucifera, also known as Indian lotus, sacred lotus.The leaves and flowers of the Lotus float and have long stems that contain air spaces in them.The lotus flowers have many petals overlapping in a proportional pattern. The root functions of lotus are carried out by rhizomes that fan out horizontally through the mud below the water. The flower is native to Asia, predominantly to the regions of India and China. A Lotus flower grows 49 inches in height and can spread 10 feet horizontally.The flower petals open in the morning and close at night. The petals of the Lotus have air-pockets to retain buoyancy on the water bodies. There are three varieties based on petal colour like white ,reddish white, and reddish are widely found all over india especially in south east.It has a very wide native distribution, ranging from central and northern India (at altitudes up to 1,400 m or 4,600 ft in the southern Himalayas),across world lotuses grows in China for about 70%, east of Iran , Australia,north America.

Etymology

The word 'nelum' which means blue is tamil word and 'nucifera' derives from latin word (nux=nut and fera=bearing)

Botany

1.) Evolution

It has a very long history (c. 3,000 years) of being cultivated for its edible seeds.Their existence stretches back some 145.5 million years.Lotus flower even survived in Ice age(time of great geological and ecological change).This hardy flower also grows naturally from Russia to Australia, China to Iran. It seems they can survive the extremes of any climate.

2.) Species

Asiatic (Nelumbo – Alba grandiflora) Growing up to four feet in height, this lotus is pure white and beautiful. It is a very large flower with a wonderful fragrance and large, pea-green foliage.American Lotus (Nelumbo lutea --> yellow)this has uncut leaves that are circular and blue-green in color. It can also get up to over three feet , Angel Wings (Nelumbo nucifera-->pink)large leaves that can get up to two feet in diameter and they are large, round, and waxy green in color , Asiatic (Nelumbo Alba grandiflora-->white) ,Star Lotus (Nymphaea nouchali-->Blue) this lotus has violet-blue petals and reddish edges.It contain 100 species. while Korea, China and Japan celebrate the flower’s beauty by holding the Lotus Festival.Today the species also occurs in southern India, Sri Lanka, virtually all of Southeast Asia, New Guinea and northern and eastern Australia, but this is probably the result of human translocations

Uses

1.) Ornamental plants

There is no much decorative use because as we cant grow lotus at indoor level as it require necessary water depth. This is used as ornamental plant for decoration in ponds and water gardens.

2.) Cut flowers

lotuses are not available as cut flowers due the fact that lotus stems are self healing.

3.) Medicinal uses

whole plant is used as astringent,emollient(haves quality of soothing the skin) diuretic, also used in treatment of diarrhoea,tissue inflammation and homeostasis(maintain body stability based on changing condition) rhizome(storage organ of lotus ) extract used as antidiabetic.It has been used for treating muscle spasms and pains.Lotus flowers are also known to be resistant to pollution, and can even purify the water they grow in. In other words, they don’t mind an acidic or dirty environment.

4.) Health benefits

In Ayurvedic Medicine lotus flowers are ground into a paste and applied to the skin, moisturizes the skin, and creating youthful looking skin. Vitamin C – Lotus flowers contain Vitamin C which is a powerful antioxidant helping to keep away diseases such as cardiovascular disease, strokes, and even cancer. When you have Vitamin B, you also get pyridoxine in the body. This component can directly interact with neural receptors in the brain to influence mental state as well as positive mood. The other negative factors such as stress levels, headaches, and irritation are also regulated.Regulates Normal Blood Pressure Potassium is found in sufficient amounts in lotus roots, which means consumption of this part can give a healthy heart.Another vitamin found in lotus roots is vitamin A, which improves the health of hair, eyes, and skin.

5.) Perfumes and food

From the stems to the rhizomes, most parts of the flower are edible. lotus fragrance is generally described as "pleasant" , "fruity", or "sweet".The lotus flowers are used for extraction of perfume. The lotus perfume available in the trade is a blend of patchouli, benzoin and styrax with phenylethyl and cinnamic alcohols.

Symbolizes

It is the national flower of India and Vietnam. It is symbol of purity,enlightment,rebirth.It is a sacred flower in both Hinduism and Buddhism, representing the path to spiritual awakening and enlightenment.The most common lotus form seen in Hinduism is the white lotus flower. Many of the gods and goddesses of Hinduism are linked to the flower; for example the goddess of prosperity, "Laxmi" is usually depicted as being seated atop a fully opened lotus flower.

Season to grow

Blooms early to late summer ,It is grown in damp soil and propogated through seed, they are cultivated in water garden.The sacred lotus requires a nutrient-rich loamy soil.In north america it bloom in mid june to july till autumn.As lotuses are day blooming plant (opening early morning closing mid afternoon and completely close in night).Lotus plants are adapted to grow in the flood plains of slow-moving rivers and delta areas. Lotus grows in a aquatic environment, sinking its tough, tuberous roots into the mud.
